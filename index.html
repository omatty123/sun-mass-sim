Understanding the physics

The Misconception

The naive model assumes Earth instantly transitions to a new circular orbit. But an orbit is a trajectory, not a shape assigned by gravity. At the moment of mass change, Earth's position and velocity are unchanged. Only the force changes. What follows is determined by the vis-viva equation, not by simple proportions.

The Critical Insight

For a circular orbit, v = √(GM/r). Escape velocity at the same distance is vesc = √(2GM/r), exactly √2 times larger. Halve M and the new escape velocity becomes √(2G·M/2/r) = √(GM/r), which equals Earth's unchanged orbital velocity. At half mass, Earth is moving at precisely escape speed.

The General Rule

If the remaining mass fraction α > 50%, Earth enters an elliptical orbit with the current point as perihelion. At exactly 50%, Earth follows a parabolic trajectory, escaping to infinity with zero residual speed. Below 50%, the escape is hyperbolic with excess velocity. The threshold is clean: exactly half.
