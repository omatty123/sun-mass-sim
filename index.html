Understanding the physics

The Misconception

Ask a physics student what happens if the Sun instantly lost half its mass and most will say the orbit would double in size. It sounds right: half the gravity, twice the radius. But this assumes a new circular orbit forms instantly, ignoring that Earth retains its original velocity. Orbits are not static shapes assigned by gravity. They are trajectories determined by both position and velocity at every moment.

The Critical Insight

For a circular orbit, v = √(GM/r). The escape velocity at the same distance is vesc = √(2GM/r), exactly √2 times larger. Now halve M: the new escape velocity becomes √(2G · M/2 / r) = √(GM/r), which equals Earth's unchanged orbital velocity. At half the Sun's mass, Earth is moving at precisely escape speed.

The General Rule

For any sudden mass loss: if the remaining fraction α > 50%, Earth enters an elliptical orbit with the current point as perihelion. At exactly 50%, Earth follows a parabolic trajectory, escaping to infinity with zero residual speed. Below 50%, the escape is hyperbolic, with excess velocity. The threshold is clean: exactly half. This is the vis-viva equation in action.
